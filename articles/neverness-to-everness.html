The Hook: A City Driven by Supernatural Anomalies

NTE takes place in Hesperia City, a breathtaking neon metropolis that feels like a cross between Tokyo and a cyberpunk fantasy. The catch? The city is plagued by anomalies—everyday objects that mutate into chaotic, gravity-defying entities due to supernatural interference.

As an Anomalist, you are tasked with investigating these strange occurrences. You'll run into oddities like floating vending machines, reality-bending traffic lights, and monsters born of urban legends. The narrative strikes a perfect balance between lighthearted city life and high-stakes supernatural mystery.

The Gameplay: Action-Packed Combat Meets Deep Simulation

What makes NTE stand out from typical action RPGs is its dual-loop gameplay:

  • Supernatural Combat: Swap between up to three characters in real-time. Combine elemental attributes like Cryo, Pyro, and Volt to trigger chain reactions that break enemy shields. Each character possesses an "Anomaly Ability" that alters the battlefield, from gravity spheres to time-warping bubbles.
  • Urban Life Simulator: Buy apartments, furnish them with fully interactive furniture, and manage your custom car collection. The driving physics are satisfyingly arcade-like, letting you drift through busy Hesperia intersections and participate in underground street races.

Pro Tip: Maximizing Team Synergies

Before launching into anomaly cleaning missions, ensure your team contains at least one shield-breaker (heavy weapon) and one elemental activator. Triggering a "Volt-Cryo" fusion instantly freezes enemies while draining their posture gauge!
