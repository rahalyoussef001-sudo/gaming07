Raid: Shadow Legends Review: Is the PC Version Still the King of Team Builders?

You've seen the memes, the sponsor banners, and the video integrations. But beneath the massive advertising campaign lies a simple truth: Raid: Shadow Legends is one of the most mechanically polished, satisfying turn-based strategy RPGs on the market today.

And if you're still playing on a small mobile screen, you are missing out on the game's true potential. The Hook: Reclaim the Kingdom of Teleria

Teleria is in ruins, corrupted by the dark lord Siroth. The Arbiter, guardian of the realm, has resurrected you—an ancient champion—to assemble an army. You'll summon, train, and equip warriors from 14 factions (including noble High Elves, savage Orcs, undead hordes, and demonic entities) to fight through campaign stages, deep dungeons, and PvP arenas.

Deep Synergy and Gear Customization

Success in Raid isn't about having the strongest single character; it's about team synergy. Every champion has specific skills, passive buffs, and aura effects. You'll need to balance your squad with:

  • Turn Meter Manipulators: Speed up your team or drain the enemy's action gauge to ensure you strike first.
  • Debuffers: Lower enemy defense or place poison effects that deal damage over time.
  • Healers/Shielders: Cleanse status ailments and protect your glass-cannon damage dealers.

Starter Champion Guide

When starting your account, select Kael as your starter champion. His area-of-effect acid rain skill and poisons make him the absolute best option for clearing the campaign quickly and farming food for other characters.

The Dungeons & Clan Boss

Once the story ends, the real game begins. You'll dive into elemental dungeons (like the Dragon's Lair or Ice Golem's Peak) to harvest rare weapons and armor. Joining a Clan lets you battle the giant Clan Boss daily, yielding chests with summon shards, skill books, and diamonds.
